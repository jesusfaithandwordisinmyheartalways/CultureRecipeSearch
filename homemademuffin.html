American HomeMade Muffin Cups

American Homemade Macaroni Muffin Cups.

The muffin cup format makes macaroni and cheese easy to transport and eat on the go, perfect for packed lunches, picnics, or parties.

They provide perfectly portioned servings, making them ideal for portion control and minimizing food waste.

Baking macaroni and cheese in muffin cups creates a higher ratio of crispy, browned edges to creamy interior, providing a delightful contrast in textures.

The interior remains creamy and cheesy, offering the same comforting mouthfeel as traditional baked macaroni and cheese.

The small size makes it easy to experiment with different add-ins like bacon bits, vegetables, or different cheeses in each cup, catering to diverse tastes.

Cuisine:American

Benefits of American Homemade Macaroni Muffin Cups?

Portion Control

  • Individual Servings: Each muffin cup is a perfect individual portion, making it easier to control serving sizes and manage caloric intake.

Convenience

  • The muffin cup format makes them easy to transport and eat on the go, making them ideal for packed lunches, picnics, and potlucks.
  • They can be prepared in advance and stored in the fridge or freezer, providing a quick and easy meal option for busy days.

Texture Variety

  • The baking process creates a higher ratio of crispy, golden-brown edges to creamy interior, adding a satisfying contrast in textures.
  • Despite the crispy edges, the interior remains creamy and cheesy, offering a delightful mouthfeel.

Versatility

  • Easily adaptable with various add-ins such as different cheeses, vegetables, meats, or spices, catering to diverse tastes and dietary preferences
  • Can be served as appetizers, snacks, side dishes, or even main courses.

Kid-Friendly

  • The fun, bite-sized format is particularly attractive to kids, making mealtime more exciting.
  • The muffin shape is easy for children to hold and eat, reducing mess and encouraging self-feeding.

Presentation

  • Macaroni muffin cups have a neat and attractive presentation, making them a hit at gatherings and parties
  • They offer a creative and novel take on traditional macaroni and cheese, making the dish feel fresh and exciting.

Consistency

  • The stovetop method reduces the risk of overcooking or drying out the dish, ensuring a consistently delicious and well-textured result every time.

Economic

  • Made with simple, affordable ingredients, macaroni muffin cups are budget-friendly and can stretch to feed a crowd
  • The individual portions help reduce food waste by allowing precise serving amounts.

Step 1:

Heat the oven to 35

Step 2:

Take a muffin tin and prepare it. Take a medium sized pot and boil water with tsp of salt. Once boiling, add elbow macaroni and cook for 8 minutes

Step 3:

Remove from heat, drain, and place back into the pot.

Step 4:

Add cheddar, cup Parmesan, cup Asiago cheese, egg, butter, remaining salt and pepper and stir well. Stir in milk.Scoop Macaroni and Cheese into the muffin tin. Once all of the tin is full, sprinkle bread crumbs and remaining cheese onto the tops.

Step 5:

Place in oven and bake for 30 minutes

Ingredients
¾ cup Stella Asiago cheese ½ cup bread crumbs 1 Tbsp butter ½ cup cheddar cheese 1 egg 2 cups elbow macaroni (uncooked) 1 cup milk ¾ cup Stella Parmesan cheese

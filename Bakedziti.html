Italian Baked Ziti

Baked ziti typically includes a flavorful combination of tomato sauce, ground meat (such as sausage or beef), garlic, onions, and Italian herbs, creating a rich and savory taste.

The blend of ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheeses adds a creamy, melty, and slightly tangy flavor that enhances the overall dish.

Baked ziti is a hearty dish that provides a sense of warmth and satisfaction, making it perfect for comfort food cravings, especially in cooler weather.

Many people associate baked ziti with family gatherings, holidays, and home-cooked meals, evoking pleasant memories and emotional comfort.

Benefits of Italian Baked Ziti?

Nutritional Value

  • Balanced Meal:Combines pasta for carbohydrates, cheese for protein and calcium, and often includes meat and vegetables, providing a well-rounded meal.
  • Vitamins and Minerals:Tomato sauce provides vitamins like A and C, as well as lycopene, a powerful antioxidant.

Comfort Food Appeal

  • The combination of pasta, meat, and cheese creates a substantial meal that is both satisfying and comforting.
  • Often associated with family gatherings and home-cooked meals, evoking feelings of warmth and nostalgia.

Flavorful and Aromatic

  • The blend of savory tomato sauce, seasoned meat, garlic, onions, and Italian herbs provides a robust and satisfying flavor profile.
  • The mixture of ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheeses adds creaminess and a slightly tangy, melty texture.

Texture Contrast

  • The interior remains creamy and rich due to the cheese and sauce mixture.
  • Baking the dish creates a golden-brown, slightly crispy cheese topping that contrasts nicely with the creamy interior.

Versatility

  • Can be adapted with different meats (such as sausage, beef, or chicken), vegetables (like spinach, bell peppers, or mushrooms), and cheeses to suit various tastes and dietary needs.
  • Works as a main course or side dish, making it versatile for family dinners, potlucks, and special gathering

Presentation

  • Macaroni muffin cups have a neat and attractive presentation, making them a hit at gatherings and parties
  • They offer a creative and novel take on traditional macaroni and cheese, making the dish feel fresh and exciting.

Convenience

  • Can be assembled in advance and baked when needed, making it a convenient option for meal planning and busy schedules
  • Can be made in large batches and frozen for future meals, providing a quick and easy dinner option on busy days.

Ease of Preparation

  • Made with common, easy-to-find ingredients, making it accessible for most home cooks.
  • The preparation process is relatively simple, involving basic cooking techniques that even novice cooks can handle.

Step 1:

Preheat oven to 35

Step 2:

Boil ziti or other pasta, i.e. rigatoni, penne, farfalle, rotini, following package directions, drain and place in a large bowl

Step 3:

To ricotta, add salt and pepper (about 1tsp each), dried parsley and blend.

Step 4:

Spray a 13x9 pan with Pam.

Step 5:

Cover the bottom half of the pan with about half the sauce

Step 6:

Put the ziti mixture on top of sauce

Step 7:

Pour remaining sauce on top of ziti

Step 8:

Sprinkle with the parmesan cheese

Step 9:

Top with the remaining mozzarella cheese.

Step 10:

Bake for 20-30 minutes until cheese is melted and it is lightly golden.

Ingredients
¾ cup Stella Asiago cheese ½ cup bread crumbs 1 Tbsp butter ½ cup cheddar cheese 1 egg 2 cups elbow macaroni (uncooked) 1 cup milk ¾ cup Stella Parmesan cheese

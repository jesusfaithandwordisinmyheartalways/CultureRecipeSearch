Italian Pasta e Fagioli (Pasta and Beans)

Italian Pasta e Fagioli (Pasta and Beans)

The combination of pasta, beans, tomatoes, and often a rich broth or sauce creates a deeply satisfying and hearty flavor.

The dish is typically seasoned with garlic, onions, rosemary, and other Italian herbs, adding aromatic and flavorful notes.

Pasta e Fagioli is a classic Italian comfort food, evoking memories of family meals and traditional cooking.

The dish combines carbohydrates from the pasta, protein from the beans, and fiber from both, making it a well-rounded and nutritious meal.

Beans are rich in essential nutrients like iron, magnesium, and potassium, while tomatoes add vitamins A and C.

Step 1:

In a large stock pan over medium-high heat, add the olive oil and the onion. Allow the onion to cook until it is tender (about 3 minutes).

Step 2:

Add the tomato (or marinara sauce) and garlic and incorporate it into the mixture. Cook for just 1-2 minutes (be careful not to burn the garlic).

Step 3:

Add the vegetable stock, water, salt, pepper and red pepper flakes. Bring this to a boil and then add your pasta. Lower the heat and allow the pasta to cook until its about 3/4 of the way finished (still slightly firm).

Step 4:

Add your beans and allow the mixture to continue cooking until the pasta is fully cooked (about 10-15 minutes).

Step 5:

Add the parsley toward the end of your cooking time. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

Step 6:

Serve in individual bowls with extra parsley as garnish and sprinkle with the Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients
2, 15-ounce cannellini beans (or Great Northern beans), rinsed and drained 1 cup of low-sodium vegetable stock 2 small tomatoes, diced (you could also use about 1/4 cup of marinara sauce) 1/4 cup diced onion 2 garlic clove, minced" 3/4 cup elbow macaroni (or any small pasta) 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated" 1 teaspoon hot pepper flakes 1 tablespoon fresh parsley (more for garnish if you'd like), torn into small pieces
